# LinkedIn Hook Writer Skill

Generate pattern-breaking hooks for LinkedIn posts using proven techniques.

## Trigger

- Manual: `/linkedin-hook-writer` or "write hooks for this"
- Pairs with: `ai-writing-audit` (run audit first, then generate hooks)

## The Hook's Job

The hook is the first 2 lines readers see before clicking "... more" on LinkedIn.

**Constraints:**
- 2 short sentences
- ~55 characters max each
- Must break scrolling pattern
- Must create an open loop (unanswered question, contradiction, bold claim)

**Anti-patterns:**
- Personal achievements ("I just hit 10K followers")
- Emoji openers (🚀 💡 ✨)
- Hashtags
- Generic statements ("Here's what I learned")
- "Let me tell you a story"

## Hook Techniques

### 1. Contradiction
Say something that sounds wrong.

```
The worst LinkedIn posts get the most followers.

Being consistent is destroying your content.
```

### 2. Specific Number + Unexpected Context
Combine a number with something surprising.

```
I mass-unfollowed 2,000 people.

My engagement tripled.

40 million views. Zero video editing skills.
```

### 3. Direct Accusation
Call the reader out.

```
You're writing LinkedIn posts for your mom.

Not your audience.

You know your posts are boring.

So does everyone scrolling past them.
```

### 4. Stolen Thought
Say what the reader secretly thinks but won't say.

```
You're tired of "thought leadership" posts.

So am I.

Nobody reads your newsletter.

You know it. I know it.
```

### 5. Absurd Reframe
Take something mundane and make it dramatic.

```
Your LinkedIn hook has 1.2 seconds to live.

Most die instantly.

I deleted my content calendar.

My best post came the next day.
```

## Workflow

### Step 1: Understand the Content

Read the audited content and identify:
- The core insight
- The pain point
- The unexpected truth
- The specific number or detail

### Step 2: Generate 10 Hooks

Create 2 hooks per technique:

```
## HOOK OPTIONS

### Technique 1: Contradiction

1A. [2-line hook]
    [2-line hook]

1B. [2-line hook]
    [2-line hook]

### Technique 2: Specific Number + Unexpected Context

2A. [2-line hook]
    [2-line hook]

2B. [2-line hook]
    [2-line hook]

[... continue for all 5 techniques]
```

### Step 3: Character Count

Verify each line is ~55 chars or less:

```
Your LinkedIn hook has 1.2 seconds to live. (42 chars) ✓
Most die instantly. (19 chars) ✓
```

### Step 4: Select Best Options

Recommend top 3 based on:
- Pattern-breaking strength
- Relevance to content
- Authenticity (matches the writer's voice)

## Example

**Input (audited content about podcast launch):**
```
Matt Karabinos wants to start a podcast for IU8. He's a one-person content team with no audio/video background.

Most organizations would hire an agency. Matt is recording next week.

Consistency beats polish. Mr. Rupp has 150K TikTok followers recording in 30-minute windows with minimal setup.

The real problem isn't the gear. It's that teachers don't know about resources because communication channels are broken.
```

**Output:**
```
## HOOK OPTIONS

### Technique 1: Contradiction

1A. The worst podcast advice is "hire a producer."
    Record next week instead.

1B. Consistency beats polish.
    But everyone optimizes for polish.

### Technique 2: Specific Number + Unexpected Context

2A. 150K TikTok followers.
    Recorded in 30-minute windows.

2B. One 20-minute recording.
    Five posts, one newsletter, endless clips.

### Technique 3: Direct Accusation

3A. You're waiting for the perfect setup.
    Your audience is already gone.

3B. Nobody reads your district emails.
    Teachers are on TikTok instead.

### Technique 4: Stolen Thought

4A. You know your resources are amazing.
    Nobody else does.

4B. "We need a communications strategy."
    You need to hit record.

### Technique 5: Absurd Reframe

5A. Your podcast has zero downloads.
    That's not the metric that matters.

5B. One person. No video background.
    Recording next week anyway.

---

## TOP 3 RECOMMENDATIONS

1. **1A** — Strong contradiction, actionable
2. **2A** — Specific number, unexpected source (TikTok educator)
3. **3B** — Direct, reveals the real problem
```

## Quality Standards

1. **Pattern-breaking** — Must stop the scroll
2. **Under 55 chars** per line — LinkedIn constraint
3. **About reader** — Not about the author's achievements
4. **No AI patterns** — No "Let's dive in" energy
5. **Authentic** — Sounds like something a real person would say

## Troubleshooting

### Hooks feel generic
- Add specific numbers
- Make the accusation sharper
- Find the unexpected detail in the content

### Hooks too long
- Cut adjectives
- Remove context (put it in the body)
- Use fragments, not complete sentences

### Hooks about the author, not reader
- Flip the perspective
- Start with "You" instead of "I"
- Focus on the reader's pain, not the author's solution
